# Minerva Benefit Intelligence Growth Strategy

**PDLC Phase:** Planning
**Status:** Draft
**Authored Date:** 03/10/2026

---

## 📝 Executive Summary

Minerva has successfully demonstrated its foundational value as a benefit quoting assistant, achieving >98% accuracy vs. the previous mid-80s manual baseline and completing UAT validation for IFP & Small Group plans. With 4 million member calls annually and benefit inquiries representing ~30% of contact volume, Minerva's goal of 2-minute handle time reduction per applicable call positions the platform to deliver measurable operational leverage at enterprise scale. This strategic objective establishes the pathway from MVP launch success to comprehensive benefit intelligence platform serving all major plan types (IFP, SG, LG, Custom) with integrated pharmacy and provider data, targeting sustainable $3M+ annual savings and >90% agent adoption across eligible benefit interactions.

## 🎯 Strategic Context & Vision

* **Company Alignment:** Direct support for Customer Experience transformation, operational efficiency, and service quality improvement through proven AI-driven accuracy gains and workflow optimization
* **Problem Statement:** Manual benefit quoting processes create accuracy risks (mid-80s %), extended handle times (+2 minutes per call), and member dissatisfaction when quotes are incomplete or incorrect, while limiting scalability of benefit support across expanding product portfolio
* **The Vision:** Deliver the enterprise-standard benefit intelligence engine powering accurate, compliant, and efficient benefit guidance across all customer touchpoints, plan types, and interaction channels, with selective member-specific capabilities in controlled use cases

## ⚠️ Constraints, Risks & Dependencies

**Dependencies:**

- Continued Nexus and Facets/Experience Cube API availability and data quality
- Systems/APIs avaiable to ingest formulary data
- Agent training capacity and customer change management support for adoption acceleration

**Risks:**

- Quality degradation as scope expands beyond initial plans and validated use-cases
- Integration complexity delays with Facets/Experience Cube and pharmacy data sources
- Agent adoption resistance or incomplete workflow integration

**Out-of-Scope:**

- Medicare, Medi-Cal, and FEP plans remain explicitly excluded until separate governance approval
- Real-time claims adjudication and authorization status (maintaining product-specific vs. member-specific boundary)
- Integration with external provider portals or member-facing digital channels beyond current CE workflow scope

**Mitigation Strategies:**

- Implement staged expansion with quality gates between each phase
- Maintain dual-track parallel operations during transition periods
- Establish rollback procedures for quality or adoption regression scenarios
